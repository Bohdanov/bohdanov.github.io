Papercraft by Nazar Bohdanov

What is Papercraft?

This is art which includes a variety of techniques to work with paper. I work in LowPoly technique, its basic principle - the maximum object recognition with a minimum polygons

You can see in the photo one of my works

A full list can be viewed by clicking on the Gallery

Is it difficult to assemble the model to beginner?

It depends on the complexity of the model chosen. Of all my scans only model of the blue marlin has a level of complexity above average and not recommended for beginners. In next paragraph I describe the process of assembling the model in this technique

This is a template

Template parts you need to cut, fold and glue

What tools will be required?

I use stationery knife and cutting board, but you can use a pair of scissors. Also need glue: ordinary PVA can be used for the first model, but it deforms the paper and dries very long. Template recommend to print on cardboard density from 160 to 300 g/m2

OK, let's get started

Each part is cut along the contour (solid line)

"Petals" on the edges of the parts are used for bonding

In the final model they won't be visible

You need to bend every part to all the bend lines

It is important not to confuse the line: the dashed line indicates the fold valley, dotted and dashed - fold mountain

How long is the process of assembling the model?

It depends on the complexity of the template, your skills and patience

And the last stage - bonding pattern parts to one another with "petals"

Each "petal" is numbered; you need to find the item, where it will be the side of the same number and stick valve on top of the number. Do not forget that all the "petals" should be hide in the finished model

It is very important to be patient

The slightest mistake can negatively affect on appearance of the model. Therefore, I ask you not pooping yourself, because neither you nor this future model will not benefit. Let modeling brings only positive emotions)))

Model is complete!!!

As you can see, there's nothing complicated. You just need patience and concentration. If these conditions are met, then the results of your work will please you for a long time
